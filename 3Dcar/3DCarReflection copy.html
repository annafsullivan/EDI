3D Car Reflection

The Project

In E&D we made 3D cars using Onshape and the 3D printer. The goal of the project was to create a one-to-one replication of a toy car. The first phase of this project was creating our car design using Onshape. We then were able to fabricate the designs using the 3D printer. Finally, each student got to be creative as they could add stickers or paint to their car. We could also be creative while designing the wooden bases for the cars, which could be painted and personalized.

The steps were as follows:

Choose and Sketch A Toy Car:

  • Each student chose a toy car to replicate, and base their design off of.
  • After choosing the car we measured our cars, and sketched its planes using its X, Y, and Z measurements. We also sketched a ¾ view of the car to help envision what it would look like in a 3D form.

Design on Onshape

  • I then created a 3D box on Onshape using the measurements of the car.
  • I pasted the sketch onto the planes of the box to help me to design the car accurately.
  • I then sketched the outline of the car and used the extrusion tool to create the shape of the car.
  • From there I created more details such as the wheels, windshield, windows, and headlights, using the Sketch and Extrusion tools.

Fabricate

  • After I was done designing the car I created a CAD Drawing and Prepared and Sliced my file.
  • In order to prepare the file for the 3D printer I transferred the Design to Ultimaker Cura. To prepare the file I created support for harsh angles so the car would print neatly.
  • From there I chose my color (red), and printed the car on the Ultimaker to create my final 3D Car design.
  • From there I added stickers to my car and created my wooden base.  

Project Guidelines

The goal of this project was to create a one-to-one replication of a toy car. One guideline was that the 3D car had to be designed as an intersection of 3 forms. Additionally, the design had to be exported as an STL, and oriented in Cura to minimize support material. Another guideline was that the file had to be exported as a gcode. Students were also expected to create and design a bubble pack for their car.

Design and Fabrication Process:

The first step of this project was to pick a toy car and measure X, Y, and Z measurements. I then created a sketch of those three planes and a ¾ view of the car. Then I created a box on Onshape with those measurements, and pasted my sketches onto the box. Then using the sketches I sketched the outlines of the car, and then removed the negative space using the extrusion tool. Next I created wheels using the circle, offset, and extrusion tools. To bring the wheels to the other sides of the car I used the transform tool and mate connectors. From there I used the sketch, plane, and extrusion features to create more detailed design on the car which included, windows, headlights, and a windshield. Additionally, I used the filet tool to round out sharp edges if necessary. Once the final design was done I created a CAD drawing and prepared my file using Cura. To prepare the file I oriented the car to minimize support, and then added necessary support. I created both an final STL file and a gcode file for printing. After the 3D printer had created the car I removed the support material and added stickers to the car. Then the final step was to create the wooden base. To create the wooden base I first had to cut and file down a piece of wood, and then I used paint and a plate for the car’s name to embellish the base.

Challenges, Setbacks, and Solutions:

One challenge that I encountered while designing my car was making the wheels of my car look the same as the toy car. On the toy car the wheels did not stick and instead were more so embedded in the car. In order to do this I had to create extrusions in the car and then build the wheels within the hollow space. The challenge that I encountered was creating the same extrusions of the other side of my car. Ms. Riley helped me figure out this problem and in order to create the extrusions on the other side I had to make the extrusions a new solid and use the Boolean tool, instead of just removing the space. Changing my method of extrusion allowed me to use the transform tool to transfer the hollow space to the other side of my car.
